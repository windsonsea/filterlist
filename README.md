# A.1 and A.2 17 Categories of Risk Keywords

English | [中文](./README-zh.md)

Based on guidelines from China’s Ministry of Industry and Information Technology and documents such as the *Internet Information Service Large Model Security Management Guide*, *Information Security Graded Protection Guide*, and *Network Content Security Specifications*, 
the classifications commonly referenced for large model service filings generally divide risks into A.1 (Core Security Risks) and A.2 (Auxiliary Security Risks), totaling 17 subcategories.

## A.1 Core Security Risks

1. [Political Sensitivity Risk](list/A.1/Political.md): Content involving national politics, political parties, leaders, policies, or other topics that may cause controversy.
2. [Violence/Terrorism Risk](list/A.1/Violence.md): Information related to terrorist activities, violent acts, and the use of weapons.
3. [Illegal/Criminal Risk](list/A.1/Criminal.md): Involves fraud, drug trafficking, theft, hacking, and other illegal activities.
4. [Extremism Risk](list/A.1/Extremism.md): Content that may promote extreme ideologies, hatred, or radical religious views.
5. [Pornography/Low-Quality Content Risk](list/A.1/Pornography.md): Adult content, vulgar material, sexual innuendo, etc.
6. [Gambling Risk](list/A.1/Gambling.md): Related to gambling, lottery cheating, or other illegal betting information.
7. [Drugs/Prohibited Substances Risk](list/A.1/Drugs.md): Content involving drugs, prohibited chemicals, or hazardous materials.
8. [Fraud/Financial Risk](list/A.1/Fraud.md): Online scams, false investment schemes, and illegal financial information.

> Different documents may vary slightly in classification, but core risks typically refer to content that **may violate laws or threaten national security or social order**.

## A.2 Auxiliary Security Risks

1. [Intellectual Property Infringement](list/A.2/Intellectual.md): Involving piracy, plagiarism, or illegal distribution of copyrighted content.
2. [Personal Privacy/Information Leakage](list/A.2/Privacy.md): Sensitive information such as ID numbers, phone numbers, home addresses, or bank account details.
3. [False Information/Rumors](list/A.2/Rumors.md): Dissemination of misleading or inaccurate content.
4. [Spam/Low-Quality Promotion](list/A.2/Spam.md): Malicious marketing, junk messages, or promotional links.
5. [Cybersecurity Risk](list/A.2/Cybersecurity.md): Providing hacking tools, vulnerability information, or illegal software.
6. [Discrimination/Hate Speech](list/A.2/Discrimination.md): Content involving discrimination or hate based on race, gender, region, religion, etc.
7. [Improper Medical/Health Information](list/A.2/Medical.md): Misleading medical, pharmaceutical, or health advice that may endanger users.
8. [Low-Quality Content/False Advertising](./list/A.2/Fake.md): Generation of misleading, repetitive, meaningless, or low-value content.
9. [Harmful Information to Minors](./list/A.2/Kid.md): Content that may negatively affect the physical or mental health of minors, including inappropriate games, early sexual innuendos, or inducement of addiction.

> Auxiliary risks generally refer to content that **may cause social impact or personal harm but does not necessarily violate the law directly**.
